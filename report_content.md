## Key Assumptions: The Foundation of Our Growth

Our financial projections are built on a set of carefully considered assumptions, allowing us to forecast performance across different scenarios (Bear, Base, Bull). These aren't guesses; they're informed estimates that drive our strategic planning.

### Customer Acquisition

*   **Monthly New Customers:** We project acquiring between 20 (Bear) and 30 (Bull) new customers each month, with a Base Case of 25. This metric is crucial for scaling our user base.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Our estimated cost to acquire a new customer ranges from $100 (Bull) to $150 (Bear), with a Base Case of $125. Optimizing CAC directly impacts our profitability.

### Revenue Drivers

*   **Average Monthly Recurring Revenue (ARPU):** Each customer is expected to generate between $18 (Bear) and $22 (Bull) in monthly revenue, with a Base Case of $20. Increasing ARPU is a key lever for revenue growth.
*   **Monthly Churn Rate:** We anticipate a monthly customer churn rate between 3% (Bull) and 8% (Bear), with a Base Case of 5%. Reducing churn is as vital as acquiring new customers for long-term stability.

### Operational Costs

*   **Variable Cost per Customer (COGS):** A consistent $2 per customer covers essential services like hosting and support.
*   **Fixed Operating Expenses:**
    *   **Sales & Marketing:** A fixed $1,500 monthly investment to drive awareness and leads.
    *   **Product Development:** $3,000 monthly dedicated to enhancing our AI nutrition coaching SaaS.
    *   **General & Administrative:** $1,000 monthly for essential overheads.

These assumptions provide a transparent view of our operational landscape, allowing us to identify areas for efficiency and investment.

## Unit Economics: The True Cost and Value of Each Customer

Understanding unit economics is paramount. It reveals the profitability of each individual customer, guiding our acquisition and retention strategies.

*   **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) & Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR):** These metrics track our predictable revenue streams, providing a clear picture of our financial health.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** As detailed above, this is the cost to bring a new customer onboard.
*   **Lifetime Value (LTV):** This critical metric calculates the total revenue we expect from a customer over their entire relationship with NutriTrack. It's derived from ARPU, Gross Margin, and Churn Rate. A high LTV indicates a healthy, sustainable business.
*   **LTV:CAC Ratio:** This ratio is the ultimate indicator of marketing efficiency. A ratio significantly above 1 suggests that for every dollar spent on acquisition, we're generating more than a dollar in lifetime value.
*   **Payback Period:** How quickly do we recoup the cost of acquiring a customer? This metric, calculated by dividing CAC by monthly gross profit per customer, highlights our cash flow efficiency.

By focusing on these unit economics, we gain a granular understanding of our business performance, enabling us to optimize for maximum profitability.
