# CAMPAIGN_STRATEGY.md

## Product/Service Overview
My Business develops and publishes engaging, casual 2D games, exemplified by titles like a Tron-style game and a Johnny Bravo joke game. The focus is on creating accessible, fun, and lighthearted entertainment experiences for a broad audience.

## Target Audience Personas

### Persona 1: Casual Gamer Chris
*   **Demographics:** Male, 25-40, office worker, lives in urban/suburban areas.
*   **Pain Points:** Seeks quick, stress-free entertainment during commutes or short breaks. Tired of complex, time-consuming games. Wants simple, pick-up-and-play experiences.
*   **Online Behavior:** Browses social media (Facebook, Instagram, Reddit) for memes and short videos. Watches casual gaming streams on YouTube. Downloads free mobile games. Engages with nostalgic content.

### Persona 2: Social Sharer Sarah
*   **Demographics:** Female, 18-30, student or young professional, active on social media.
*   **Pain Points:** Looks for fun, shareable content to engage with friends online. Wants games that are easy to understand and have humorous or visually appealing moments to post.
*   **Online Behavior:** Highly active on TikTok, Instagram, and Twitter/X. Shares funny videos, memes, and short game clips. Follows influencers and trending topics. Enjoys lighthearted challenges and quizzes.

## Competitor Analysis

### 1. Voodoo
*   **Positioning:** Hyper-casual mobile game publisher. Focus on simple mechanics, addictive loops, and mass market appeal.
*   **Content:** Short, engaging video ads showcasing gameplay. Focus on immediate gratification and ease of play. Heavy use of A/B testing for ad creatives.

### 2. Ketchapp
*   **Positioning:** Casual mobile game developer known for minimalist design and challenging, yet simple, gameplay.
*   **Content:** Gameplay trailers, high score challenges, visually appealing screenshots. Promotes competitive aspects and replayability.

### 3. Playgendary
*   **Positioning:** Developer of popular casual games with strong character design and engaging narratives (for casual games).
*   **Content:** Animated trailers, character spotlights, in-game event promotions. Emphasizes fun, quirky characters and progression.

## Platform Strategy

*   **TikTok:** Primary platform for short, viral gameplay clips, humorous moments, and challenge videos. High organic reach potential.
    *   **Best Practices:** Use trending sounds, quick cuts, text overlays, call-to-actions for download/play. Engage with comments.
*   **Instagram (Reels/Stories/Carousels):** Visual storytelling, behind-the-scenes, character art, short gameplay snippets, interactive polls.
    *   **Best Practices:** High-quality visuals, engaging captions, use of relevant hashtags, interactive elements in stories.
*   **YouTube (Shorts/Long-form):** Gameplay trailers, developer diaries, "how-to-play" guides, compilation of funny moments, influencer collaborations.
    *   **Best Practices:** Optimize for search, clear thumbnails, strong hooks, consistent upload schedule.
*   **Twitter/X:** Quick updates, announcements, community engagement, GIF-based gameplay snippets, polls, direct interaction with players.
    *   **Best Practices:** Concise messaging, use of relevant hashtags, engaging visuals, timely responses.

## Messaging Pillars
1.  **Instant Fun & Easy Play:** Emphasize quick, accessible entertainment with simple controls.
2.  **Nostalgic Charm:** Leverage retro aesthetics or classic game mechanics for a sense of familiarity and warmth.
3.  **Shareable Moments:** Highlight humorous, surprising, or visually appealing gameplay that encourages social sharing.
4.  **Lighthearted Escape:** Position games as a perfect way to unwind and de-stress with a smile.

## Tone of Voice Guidelines
*   **Adjectives:** Playful, Witty, Engaging, Enthusiastic, Approachable.
*   **Do:** Use lighthearted humor, emojis, conversational language, encourage interaction, celebrate player achievements.
*   **Don't:** Be overly serious, use complex jargon, sound corporate, be condescending, or overly pushy with sales.

## Content Types per Platform
*   **TikTok:** Short gameplay loops (5-15s), reaction videos, "POV" style content, challenge prompts, meme-based videos.
*   **Instagram:** Reels (gameplay highlights, character intros), Stories (polls, Q&A, behind-the-scenes), Carousels (game art, tips & tricks, "meet the team").
*   **YouTube:** Game trailers (30-60s), "Let's Play" snippets, developer commentary, community highlight reels.
*   **Twitter/X:** Text-based announcements, GIF gameplay, polls, "Question of the Day," fan art showcases.

## Posting Cadence
*   **TikTok:** 3-5 times per week
*   **Instagram:** 3-4 times per week (mix of Reels, Stories, Posts)
*   **YouTube:** 1-2 times per week (mix of Shorts and longer content)
*   **Twitter/X:** 4-6 times per week
