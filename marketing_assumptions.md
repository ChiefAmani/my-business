# Marketing Assumptions for "My Business" Financial Model

This document provides initial marketing-related assumptions for the "My Business" financial model, as outlined in `DATA_SPEC.md`. These values are preliminary estimates based on general industry benchmarks for online businesses and will require further validation through market research and initial campaign testing.

## Assumptions Sheet Inputs (Marketing Focus)

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
*   **Description:** The average cost to acquire one new customer through marketing and sales efforts.
*   **Rationale:** Assumes a mix of digital marketing channels (e.g., paid social, search ads, content marketing) with varying efficiencies.
*   **Scenario Estimates:**
    *   **Bear:** $75 (Higher competition, less optimized campaigns, lower conversion rates)
    *   **Base:** $50 (Reasonable performance with ongoing optimization)
    *   **Bull:** $35 (Highly effective campaigns, strong organic growth, high conversion rates)

### Monthly Customers Acquired
*   **Description:** The number of new customers acquired each month. This is a key driver of revenue growth.
*   **Rationale:** Reflects the scale of marketing efforts and market demand.
*   **Scenario Estimates:**
    *   **Bear:** 80 units (Limited marketing spend, lower market interest)
    *   **Base:** 100 units (Consistent marketing efforts, steady market interest)
    *   **Bull:** 150 units (Aggressive marketing, strong market demand, viral growth potential)

### Churn Rate
*   **Description:** The percentage of existing customers lost each month. Lower churn indicates higher customer retention and lifetime value.
*   **Rationale:** Assumes a typical SaaS or subscription-based service, where customer satisfaction and ongoing value are critical.
*   **Scenario Estimates:**
    *   **Bear:** 7% (Poor product-market fit, inadequate customer support, high competition)
    *   **Base:** 5% (Acceptable retention, some customer loss due to natural attrition or minor issues)
    *   **Bull:** 3% (Strong product, excellent customer service, high customer loyalty)

### Average Selling Price (ASP)
*   **Description:** The average revenue generated per unit or customer per month.
*   **Rationale:** This is a critical input for revenue calculations and is often influenced by pricing strategy and perceived value, which marketing communicates.
*   **Scenario Estimates:**
    *   **Bear:** $90 (Price sensitivity, discounting, lower perceived value)
    *   **Base:** $100 (Standard pricing, good value proposition)
    *   **Bull:** $110 (Premium pricing, strong brand, high perceived value)

## Next Steps

These assumptions should be integrated into the financial model spreadsheet. As the project progresses, these values must be refined based on actual marketing campaign performance data, A/B testing, and ongoing market research. The CMO will work with the team to track and optimize these metrics to improve the accuracy and performance of the business model.
